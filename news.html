Kings unlikely to renew Bayliss contract, seek an Indian coach

After Ricky Ponting, another Australian is set to take a bow from the elite coaching group in the Indian Premier League (IPL). Trevor Bayliss, the head coach of the Punjab Kings franchise, could also be on his way out. His two-year contract with the Punjab Kings has ended, and the franchise is unlikely to renew it.

Dushmantha Chameera ruled out of India T20Is

Sri Lankan fast bowler Dushmantha Chameera has been ruled out of the India series, which begins on July 27. According to an SLC media release, "Chameera is still recovering from a bronchitis and respiratory infection he contracted, and hence will not be part of the T20I series."

Harry Brook wants 'to be Harry Brook, not anybody else'

Harry Brook is undeniably one of the rising stars in English cricket. This summer, he showcased his potential by scoring a half-century at Lord's and a century at Trent Bridge against the West Indies. Despite his achievements, the 25-year-old remains humble, dismissing comparisons with former greats and focusing on carving out his own unique path in the sport.

Ireland, Zimbabwe gear up for historic Test in Belfast

It's not often that Ireland and Zimbabwe get to play Test cricket, and this will be the first ever red-ball encounter between the two sides as Ireland gears up to host Zimbabwe for the one-off Test match in Belfast, starting on Thursday (July 25). Rust is inevitable for the tourists who haven't featured in the longest format since February 2023. They are also grappling with a squad that is short on experience with a plethora of young talent who've only recently made it to the Test squad.

Stoinis, du Plessis fire TSK to Eliminator win over MI New York

When the blue and yellow face off, there is a lot of fanfare irrespective of the tournament and this showdown was no different. It was the Texas Super Kings who cantered home and won by nine wickets to knock holders MI New York out of MLC 2024. Marcus Stoinis (2 for 18) was instrumental with ball in hand while Faf du Plessis (72 off 47) and Devon Conway (51 off 43) led the chase with a 101-run stand in the chase.
